Circular Life

This is a mutant example of Conway's Game of Life, except in a circular fashion instead of a grid. As the radial layers gets larger, so does the amount of nodes. Therefore the grid could be envisioned as a toroidal cone instead. The rules being used are simpler than the original game.

  • Exactly two living neighbors will cause this node to live to the next cycle.
  • Three or more living neighbors will cause this node to die upon the next cycle.
Finally, clicking and dragging the mouse away from the center of the screen will run this rendition of the Game of Life will cycle in different intervals, depending on the angle of each node in the layer. The larger the layer, the faster the life scanning cycle will occur.

This project comes with audio - whenever each layer is scanned, a frequency is played using an oscilator. The frequency depends on the number of living cells at that certain calculation. You can drag the screen with mouse clicks to make the radius smaller or larger, which effects the volume of the audio as well.
